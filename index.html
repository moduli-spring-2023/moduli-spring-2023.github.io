Polynomial Invariant Counter

This program counts the number of invariants (barring caveats about overflow) in the classical action of GL on the set of polynomials in n variables over a finite field of order p^m of degree at most d. The algorithm used is described[[link pending]]. This is a(n unforunately single threaded) web wrapper for a tool written for research under theMason Experimental Geometry Labin Spring 2023 by Holly Miller and Violet Nguyen.

Usage:
d - maximum total x-degree of desired polynomials
n - number of variables
p - prime where the order of the field is p^m
m - exponent where the order of the field is p^m

Note: Even small values may overflow the polynomial count. This may also melt your device for large degree.
